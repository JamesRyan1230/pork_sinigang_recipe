PORK SINIGANG

Sinigang na Baboy is one of the most beloved Filipino dishes,known for its rich,meaty flavor and distinct sourness.

This classic pork stew is packed with fresh vegetables,including daikon radish,okra,eggplant,and string beans,all simmered in a tamarid-based broth.

Its combination of ingredents makes it a healthy and nutritious meal,perfect for a satisfying and comforting dish.

COOKING METHOD

Sinigang is cooked by stewing,a method that retains the natural flavors and nutrients without requiring excesssive oil.

Unlike deep-fried dishes,this approach helps reduce unhealthy fats and calories.

HOW TO COOK SINIGANG NA BABOY

INGREDIENTS:


  • 2 lbs. pork belly
  • 40 grams sinigang mix
  • 1 eggplant,sliced
  • 8 okras
  • 12 string beans,cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 2 long green peppers
  • 1 tomato,wedged
  • 1 onion,wedged
  • 3 taro roots
  • 3 oz. daikon radish
  • 6 cups water
  • 3 tbsp. cooking oil
  • Fish sauce and ground black pepper to taste

    • COOOKING INSTRUCTIONS:

    PROCEDURE:

    1. Saute Aromatics: Heat oil in a large pot.Saute the union and half of the tomatoes until the union softens.
    2. Brown the Pork: Add the pork belly and cook until it turns light brown.
    3. Boil the Meat: Pour in water and bring to a boil.Add the daikon radish and taro roots.Cover and let it simmer over low heat until the pork is tender.
    4. Add Seasoning and Vegetables: Stir in the sinigang mix and eggplant,then cook for 5 minutes.
    5. Incorporate Remaining Ingredients: Add long green peppers, okra,string beans,and the rest of the tomato.Cook for another 5-8 minutes,ensuring the vegetables stay crisp.
    6. Final Touch: Season with fish sauce and ground black pepper.Add spinach,turn off the heat,and let it sit covered for 2 minutes before serving.

    Storing Sinigang na Baboy

    To keep leftovers fresh,allow the sinigang to cool completely before transferring it to an airtight container.Store it in the refrigerator for up to 2 days.
